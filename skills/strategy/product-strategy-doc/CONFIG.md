# Customizing This Skill

## Quick Start

This skill works out of the box to generate comprehensive product strategy documents. No configuration is required to start using it. However, you can enhance the skill's output by customizing a few settings to match your organization's terminology, decision-making processes, and documentation standards.

## What to Customize

### 1. Document Metadata Fields

**What to look for:** The template header in section "Step 2: Strategy Doc Structure" includes standard fields like Author, Date, Status, and Audience.

**Why customize:** Your organization may track additional metadata (e.g., version numbers, approval stage, document owner) or use different status labels.

**Example:**
- **Generic:** `**Status:** Draft / In Review / Approved`
- **Customized:** `**Status:** Draft / Peer Review / Leadership Review / Board Approved` or add `**Version:** 2.3` and `**Doc Owner:** [PM Name]`

### 2. Audience Types

**What to look for:** References to "Team / Leadership / Board" as audience options throughout the skill.

**Why customize:** Your organization may have specific stakeholder groups (e.g., "Product Council," "Investment Committee," "Regional GMs") that should be named explicitly.

**Example:**
- **Generic:** `**Audience:** [Team / Leadership / Board]`
- **Customized:** `**Audience:** [Product Council / Executive Staff / Board of Directors]`

### 3. Review Cadence

**What to look for:** Section 9 suggests "Monthly: Leading indicator check / Quarterly: Assumptions review / Annually: Full strategy refresh."

**Why customize:** Your planning cycles may differ. Some organizations do quarterly business reviews, others use rolling 6-month planning windows.

**Example:**
- **Generic:** `Monthly: Leading indicator check`
- **Customized:** `Bi-weekly: KPI dashboard review` or `Each QBR: Strategy health check`

### 4. Metric Terminology

**What to look for:** References to "OKR key results" and general metric categories.

**Why customize:** Your organization may use different goal-setting frameworks (V2MOM, Rocks, 4DX) or have specific metric naming conventions.

**Example:**
- **Generic:** "distinct from feature metrics or OKR key results"
- **Customized:** "distinct from sprint metrics or quarterly Rocks" or "different from V2MOM measures"

## Tips

1. **Keep a local configuration section:** Add a "Local Conventions" subsection at the end of Step 2 where you document your organization-specific terminology. This makes it easy to update when the skill itself gets refreshed.

2. **Align with existing templates:** If your company already has a strategy doc template or format, adapt section ordering and naming in Step 2 to match — this reduces cognitive overhead for your stakeholders.

3. **Customize the Quality Checklist:** Section "Step 5: Quality Checklist" can be extended with your organization's specific requirements (e.g., "Links to supporting analysis in [your BI tool]," "Reviewed by Finance for revenue impact").

The skill is designed to be universally applicable while remaining flexible enough to reflect how your specific organization thinks about and documents product strategy.
