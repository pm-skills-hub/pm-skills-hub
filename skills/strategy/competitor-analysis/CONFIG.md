# Customizing This Skill

## Quick Start

This competitive analysis skill works out of the box for any product manager. It produces rigorous competitive analyses, market maps, feature matrices, and sales battlecards without requiring any setup.

However, you can significantly increase its usefulness by customizing it to reflect your team's specific context, competitive landscape, and analytical frameworks.

## What to Customize

### 1. Competitor Categories

The skill uses four competitor types (Direct, Indirect, Adjacent, Emerging). You may want to adjust these categories to match how your team thinks about competition.

**Why customize:** Your market may have unique competitive dynamics (e.g., open source vs. commercial, regional vs. global, horizontal vs. vertical-specific) that don't fit neatly into generic categories.

**Example customization:**
- Generic: "Adjacent competitors: Different primary job, but overlapping use cases"
- Personalized: "Platform competitors: Companies with broader platforms that include our use case as one module (e.g., Salesforce, ServiceNow, Microsoft)"

### 2. Market Map Dimensions

The skill suggests dimension pairs like "Breadth vs. Depth" or "Enterprise vs. SMB." Substitute dimensions that reveal strategic differentiation in YOUR market.

**Why customize:** The most useful market maps highlight the axes of competition that actually matter for positioning decisions in your space.

**Example customization:**
- Generic: "Self-serve / SMB-focused vs. sales-led / enterprise-focused"
- Personalized: "Developer-first vs. Business-user-first" (if you're in DevTools) or "Cloud-native vs. On-prem capable" (if you're in infrastructure)

### 3. Feature Matrix Dimensions

The skill says "Max 15-20 dimensions" and groups by Core/Advanced/Platform. Define what those categories mean for your product area.

**Why customize:** A generic feature matrix produces generic insights. Tailoring dimensions to your product's key differentiation areas makes the analysis actionable.

**Example customization:**
- Generic category: "**AI / ML**"
- Personalized category: "**Workflow Automation**" (if automation is your core value prop) or "**Data Governance & Lineage**" (if you're in data tooling)

### 4. Moat Assessment Criteria

The skill includes seven moat types. Add or emphasize moat types specific to your market.

**Why customize:** Different markets have different defensibility dynamics. A marketplace has network effects; an infrastructure tool has integration lock-in; a compliance tool has regulatory moats.

**Example customization:**
- Add: "Marketplace liquidity" (for two-sided platforms)
- Add: "Dataset/model quality" (for AI/ML products)
- De-emphasize: "Regulatory / compliance" (if not relevant to your market)

### 5. Strategic Implications Timeframes

The skill uses "Immediate (next quarter)" and "Medium-term (6-18 months)." Adjust these to match your planning cycles.

**Why customize:** Different organizations plan on different horizons. Enterprise SaaS may think in annual cycles; consumer tech may think in monthly sprints.

**Example customization:**
- Generic: "Medium-term implications (6-18 months)"
- Personalized: "Next fiscal year (FY25 planning)" or "H2 roadmap decisions"

## Tips

**Keep a customization log:** Maintain a separate section at the top of your SKILL.md file labeled "## Team-Specific Customizations" where you document all changes. This makes it easier to merge updates when new versions of the skill are released.

**Align with existing frameworks:** If your team already uses specific competitive analysis templates (e.g., Porter's Five Forces, Blue Ocean Strategy Canvas), reference those frameworks explicitly in the skill to maintain consistency with your team's existing mental models.

**Update with win/loss learnings:** As you conduct competitive analyses and gather win/loss data, refine the "Win / Loss Patterns" section with actual patterns you're seeing in your deals. Real data makes the template exponentially more useful over time.
