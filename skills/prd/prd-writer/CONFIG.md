# Customizing This Skill

## Quick Start

This PRD skill works out of the box and follows industry-standard product documentation practices. However, you can tailor it to match your team's specific PRD format, terminology, and workflows for even better results.

## What to Customize

### 1. PRD Template Structure

**What to look for:** The section headings and order in "Step 2: PRD Structure"

**Why customize:** Your team may use different section names, combine certain sections, or add custom sections (like "Customer Quotes" or "Legal Review").

**Example:**
- Generic: "6. Non-Functional Requirements"
- Personalized: "6. Quality Attributes" (if your team uses different terminology)

### 2. Status Values

**What to look for:** The Status line in the PRD header ("Draft | In Review | Approved")

**Why customize:** Your workflow may use different status labels that match your document approval process.

**Example:**
- Generic: "Draft | In Review | Approved"
- Personalized: "Draft | Tech Review | Stakeholder Review | Approved | In Development"

### 3. Prioritization Framework

**What to look for:** Section 5 uses "MoSCoW" (Must/Should/Could/Won't Have)

**Why customize:** Your team might prefer P0/P1/P2, RICE scoring, or another framework.

**Example:**
- Generic: "Must Have (M)" / "Should Have (S)"
- Personalized: "P0 (Launch Blocker)" / "P1 (High Priority)" / "P2 (Backlog)"

### 4. Metric Timeframes

**What to look for:** Section 9 mentions "7-day, 30-day, 90-day" evaluation periods

**Why customize:** Adjust these to match your release cycle and metric review cadence.

**Example:**
- Generic: "7-day, 30-day, 90-day"
- Personalized: "Week 1, Sprint End, Quarter End" (for teams on 2-week sprints)

## Tips

- Keep a team copy of this skill with your customizations applied, and note which sections you've modified in a comment at the top
- If your PRD format changes, update the "PRD Structure" section first — it's the blueprint for all generated documents
- Consider adding your team's PRD review checklist as an additional section if it differs from the quality checklist provided
