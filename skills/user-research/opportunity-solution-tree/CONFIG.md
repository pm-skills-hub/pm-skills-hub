# Customizing This Skill

## Quick Start

This skill works out of the box and follows Teresa Torres' standard Opportunity Solution Tree framework. However, you can customize it to align with your team's terminology, discovery process, and tooling preferences.

## What to Customize

### 1. Experiment Types

**What to look for:** The "Experiment Types" section lists five common experiment approaches (fake door, concierge, prototype test, A/B test, staged rollout).

**Why customize:** Your team may use different experiment terminology or have additional lightweight testing methods unique to your org.

**Example:**
- Generic: "Concierge: Manually do the thing the product would automate"
- Customized: "Wizard of Oz test: Use internal tools to simulate the feature for a pilot customer"

### 2. OST Review Process

**What to look for:** Step 8 mentions "Workshop agenda: How to run an OST review session (30-60 min)" but doesn't specify your team's actual process.

**Why customize:** Add your team's specific review cadence, stakeholders, and decision-making framework.

**Example:**
- Generic: "Who should review this tree? (PM, Design, Eng, leadership)"
- Customized: "Present to Product Trio (PM + Designer + Tech Lead) in weekly discovery sync, then share async in #product-discovery for broader feedback"

### 3. Outcome Format

**What to look for:** The skill recommends outcomes as metrics with direction ("Increase 30-day activation rate").

**Why customize:** Your organization may tie outcomes to specific OKR formats, planning cycles, or metric dashboards.

**Example:**
- Generic: "Connected to: [Business goal or OKR]"
- Customized: "Connected to Q2 OKR 1.3 — link to team OKR doc: [your planning tool URL]"

### 4. Diagram Rendering

**What to look for:** The Mermaid diagram section assumes Markdown-based tools.

**Why customize:** Specify where your team actually renders these diagrams (Notion, Confluence, GitHub, Miro, etc.) and any org-specific formatting preferences.

**Example:**
- Generic: "Useful for team reviews, Notion pages, or stakeholder presentations"
- Customized: "Paste into Confluence using the Mermaid macro, or export as PNG for leadership decks using [your preferred tool]"

## Tips

- Keep customizations in a team "OST Playbook" doc that references this skill — this makes it easier to update when the skill gets new features
- If your team has a standard OST template in Notion/Confluence, link to it in the skill's "Output Format" section
- Pair this skill with your team's continuous discovery ritual (interview cadence, snapshot walls, research repo) for maximum impact
