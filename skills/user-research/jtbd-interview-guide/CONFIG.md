# Customizing This Skill

## Quick Start

This skill works out of the box to generate JTBD interview guides, screeners, and synthesis templates. No customization is required to start using it. However, personalizing a few elements will make the output feel more natural for your team's research process and improve adoption.

## What to Customize

### 1. Interview Logistics & Tools

**What to look for:** The skill mentions "Live video call, in-person, async written" formats and references "account data" and "support tickets."

**Why customize:** Your team may have standard tools (Zoom, Gong, UserTesting) or specific data sources (CRM fields, analytics dashboards) that should be referenced consistently.

**Example:**
- Generic: "Review the customer's account data"
- Personalized: "Pull their usage data from Amplitude and Salesforce account history"

### 2. Research Cadence & Volume

**What to look for:** The skill suggests "6-8 interviews to reach saturation" and "at least 30 days" of product usage.

**Why customize:** Your research velocity and customer lifecycle may differ. B2B enterprise might need longer usage periods; high-velocity consumer products might saturate faster.

**Example:**
- Generic: "Has been using the product for at least 30 days"
- Personalized: "Has completed at least 2 billing cycles (60 days minimum)"

### 3. Integration with Your OST Practice

**What to look for:** The skill mentions pairing with "opportunity-solution-tree skill" and mapping to "OST opportunity space."

**Why customize:** If your team uses different frameworks (Impact Mapping, North Star, etc.) or has a specific OST template, reference those instead.

**Example:**
- Generic: "Map findings to opportunity space"
- Personalized: "Map findings to our Q2 Opportunity Space Canvas in Miro"

## Tips

- **Keep a local config section:** Add a "Team Defaults" section at the top of your customized version with your standard screener criteria, tools, and research ops contacts.
- **Version your templates:** When you customize the synthesis template, save it as a separate file so future skill updates don't overwrite your format.
- **Document your deviations:** If you adjust interview lengths or sample sizes based on your domain, note why — it helps onboard new researchers.
