# Customizing This Skill

## Quick Start

This skill works out of the box to generate professional weekly product emails. However, personalizing a few elements will make the output feel native to your team's voice and format.

## What to Customize

### 1. Newsletter Name

**What to look for:** The skill asks for a "cadence name" and uses generic placeholders like "[NEWSLETTER NAME]".

**Why customize:** A memorable name builds brand recognition and makes your emails more anticipated. It signals "this is our thing."

**Example:**
- Generic: "Product Update — Week of [Date]"
- Personalized: "The Weekly Build" or "Ship It Friday" or "Product Pulse"

### 2. Tone Preferences

**What to look for:** The skill asks about tone (professional vs. conversational) but you can set a default in your local version.

**Why customize:** Encoding your team's default tone saves setup time each week.

**Example:**
- Add a note in Step 1: "Default tone for our team: conversational and fun with occasional emoji"
- Or: "Default tone: professional but approachable, minimal emoji"

### 3. Section Defaults

**What to look for:** The email structure includes optional sections like "Team Wins & Shoutouts" and "Trends We're Watching".

**Why customize:** Your team may always include certain sections or never use others. Document your preferences.

**Example:**
- Add: "Always include: Roadmap Highlights, Feature Spotlight, Coming Up"
- Add: "Skip by default: Trends (only include when significant)"

### 4. Length Preference

**What to look for:** The skill asks for "Approximate length: Short (300-400 words) or full-length (600-900 words)?"

**Why customize:** If your audience consistently prefers one format, set it as default.

**Example:**
- Add: "Our standard: Short format (300-400 words) for better engagement"

## Tips

- **Keep a template:** Save a personalized version of the email structure in your team wiki so new PMs can follow the format.
- **Version control:** If you modify the skill file directly, keep your customizations in clearly marked sections so they're easy to preserve when the skill updates.
- **Experiment:** Try different subject line styles for a month and track open rates to find what resonates with your audience.
