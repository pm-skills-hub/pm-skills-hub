# Customizing This Skill

## Quick Start

This skill works immediately without customization. It's designed to help you create executive and board-level product summaries that communicate at the right altitude — focusing on business impact, strategic progress, and risks rather than features and tasks.

However, personalizing a few elements will make the output feel more native to your organization and audience.

## What to Customize

### 1. Metrics That Matter

**What to look for:** The skill references generic metrics like "ARR, activation, retention, NPS, usage, platform health" in Step 2.

**Why customize:** Your executives and board likely have 2-3 metrics they care about above all others — often tied to your specific business model, stage, or strategic priorities.

**Example:**
- **Generic:** "What metrics matter most to this audience? (ARR, activation, retention, NPS, usage, platform health)"
- **Personalized:** "What metrics matter most to this audience? (MRR growth rate, logo retention, product-qualified leads, platform uptime, time-to-value)"

### 2. Audience Types

**What to look for:** The skill lists audience options like "CEO/C-suite, board of directors, investors, cross-functional leadership, steering committee" in Step 2.

**Why customize:** Add or rename audience types that match your organization's structure. For example, you might have a Product Council, Operating Committee, or specific investor update cadences.

**Example:**
- **Generic:** "What is the audience? (CEO/C-suite, board of directors, investors, cross-functional leadership, steering committee)"
- **Personalized:** "What is the audience? (CEO/C-suite, board of directors, Product Council, Operating Committee, Series B investors)"

### 3. Meeting Cadences

**What to look for:** The skill mentions occasions like "QBR, board meeting, monthly business review, ad hoc update, fundraising deck section" in Step 2.

**Why customize:** Align these with your actual meeting rhythms and naming conventions.

**Example:**
- **Generic:** "What is the occasion? (QBR, board meeting, monthly business review, ad hoc update, fundraising deck section)"
- **Personalized:** "What is the occasion? (Quarterly Board Meeting, Monthly ELT Review, Investor Update, Strategic Planning Session, Product Ops Review)"

### 4. Status Indicators

**What to look for:** The skill uses emoji indicators (🟢 / 🟡 / 🔴) in Format B metric tables.

**Why customize:** Some organizations use different conventions — text labels (On Track / At Risk / Behind), symbols (✓ / ⚠ / ✗), or colors only.

**Example:**
- **Generic:** "| Metric | Last Period | This Period | Target | Trend |\n|---|---|---|---|---|\n| [Metric 1] | | | | 🟢 / 🟡 / 🔴 |"
- **Personalized:** "| Metric | Last Period | This Period | Target | Status |\n|---|---|---|---|---|\n| [Metric 1] | | | | On Track / At Risk / Behind |"

## Tips

- **Keep a template:** Once you've personalized the prompts in Step 2, save them in a separate note. This makes it easy to copy-paste your customized context questions each time you use the skill.
- **Update metrics quarterly:** As your business evolves, the metrics that matter to leadership often shift. Review and update your customized metric list at the start of each quarter.
- **Version for different audiences:** Consider maintaining light variations of this skill for different audiences (e.g., one version tuned for board meetings, another for monthly CEO updates) if their needs diverge significantly.
