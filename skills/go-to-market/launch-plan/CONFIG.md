# Customizing This Skill

## Quick Start

This launch plan skill works out of the box for any product manager planning a feature or product release. No customization is required to start using it.

However, personalizing a few elements will make the plans more actionable for your specific team and workflow.

## What to Customize

### 1. Launch Tier Definitions

**What to look for:** Section titled "Launch Tier Framework" with four tiers of launch activities.

**Why customize:** Your organization may have different internal terminology ("P0/P1/P2" instead of "Tier 1/2/3"), different activities for each tier, or different approval requirements.

**Example:**
- **Generic:** "Tier 1 — Major Launch" with press and analyst briefings
- **Personalized:** "P0 Launch — requires exec sponsor, analyst briefing through corporate comms team, and legal sign-off from Jane Smith"

### 2. Channel Plan Options

**What to look for:** Table in "Channel Plan" section listing marketing and communication channels.

**Why customize:** Add your team's specific channels or remove ones you don't use. Include channel-specific details like Slack workspace names, blog platform, or CRM system.

**Example:**
- **Generic:** "Internal Slack" and "Help center / docs"
- **Personalized:** "#product-announcements Slack channel" and "Zendesk Help Center (help.yourcompany.com)"

### 3. Stakeholder Teams

**What to look for:** "Stakeholder Enablement" section with Sales, CS, Marketing, and Engineering subsections.

**Why customize:** Add or remove teams based on your organization structure. Some companies have separate Product Marketing, DevRel, or Operations teams.

**Example:**
- **Generic:** "Customer Success / Support"
- **Personalized:** "Customer Success (led by Sarah Chen)" and "Support Engineering (escalation via #support-eng)"

### 4. Success Metrics Template

**What to look for:** Table in "Success Metrics" section with measurement timeframes.

**Why customize:** Adjust measurement windows to match your team's sprint or review cadence. Add your company's standard metrics framework.

**Example:**
- **Generic:** "T+30" measurement date
- **Personalized:** "End of Q2" or "Sprint 47 retro" aligned with your planning cycle

## Tips

- Keep customizations in a separate "Team Conventions" section at the bottom of SKILL.md for easier updates when new versions are released
- Create a companion template in your project management tool (Jira, Asana, Notion) that mirrors this structure with your team's specific fields
- Share your customized version with your team in a shared location so everyone uses consistent launch planning frameworks
